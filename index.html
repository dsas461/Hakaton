1957: The Soviet Union launches Sputnik, the first artificial satellite.

1961: Soviet cosmonaut Yuri Gagarin becomes the first person to orbit the Earth.

1969: American astronaut Neil Armstrong becomes the first person to walk on the moon during the Apollo 11 mission.

1971: The Soviet Union launches the first space station, Salyut 1.

1986: The Space Shuttle Challenger explodes shortly after launch, killing all seven crew members.

1998: The International Space Station (ISS) is launched and begins continuous human occupancy.

2004: The Mars Exploration Rovers, Spirit and Opportunity, begin exploring the surface of Mars.

2012: NASA's Curiosity rover lands on Mars and begins a mission to study the planet's geology and climate.

2021: NASA's Perseverance rover lands on Mars with a mission to search for signs of ancient microbial life.

Davinci

NASA's DAVINCI mission will launch in the late 2020s to explore Venus from near the top of the clouds to the planet’s surface.

Veritas

VERITAS will be the first NASA spacecraft to explore Earth’s sister planet Venus since the 1990s.

Mars Sample Return

Mars Sample Return is a proposed mission to bring samples collected by NASA's Mars Perseverance rover to Earth.
